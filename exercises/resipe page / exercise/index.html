Pumpkin Churros

Dessert

  • Prep Time: 30 minutes
  • Time: 30 minutes
  • Total Time: 1 hour
  • Yields: 10 to 12 churros

Pumpkin season is here, and I had to turn one of my favorite fall flavors into something fun and delicious! These pumpkin-shaped churros are crispy, warm, and loaded with pumpkin spice goodness—basically fall in every bite. Trust me, you’ll want to make a batch because they’ll disappear in seconds!

Pumpkin Pie Churros

Pumpkin season is here, and I had to turn one of my favorite fall flavors into something fun and delicious! These pumpkin-shaped churros are crispy, warm, and loaded with pumpkin spice goodness—basically fall in every bite. Trust me, you’ll want to make a batch because they’ll disappear in seconds!

Ingredients

CINNAMON SUGAR MIXTURE

  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 teaspoon pumpkin pie spice

    • CHURRO BATTER

  • 3/4 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons brown sugar
  • 1/2 tablespoon vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup pumpkin puree
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • vegetable oil, for frying

    • OPTIONAL DIPPING SAUCES

      caramel sauce
      chocolate sauce
      sweetened condensed milk

    Instructions

    1. In a small bowl, whisk together sugar and cinnamon to make the cinnamon-sugar mixture. Set aside.
    2. In a pot, add water, sugar, vanilla extract, pumpkin puree, unsalted butter, and salt.
    3. Set over medium-high heat and whisk together.
      4. Once it comes to a boil, drop the heat to low and add the flour. Use a rubber spatula to mix together until there are no more clumps of flour left, about 3 minutes.
    4. Take off the heat and allow to cool for 6-8 minutes.
    5. Add one egg and mix using a rubber spatula or in a stand mixer until fully incorporated. Add the second egg and mix again until fully incorporated. The dough should be firm, elastic and just a bit sticky.
    6. Transfer into a piping bag fitted with a tip.
    7. Cut parchment paper into small squares. Spray with oil and pipe pumpkins out onto each square. You may need to use scissors to cut the stem off at the end.
    8. Heat oil to 325°F and drop one heart in at a time, making sure to move slowly so it drops into the oil smoothly. Allow it to get golden brown on one side before flipping over. Once it’s golden on both sides, transfer to a paper towel to drain excess oil. Repeat until all the churros are fried.
    9. Coat the churros in the cinnamon sugar mixture.
    10. Enjoy with some caramel or chocolate sauce!
